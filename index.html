Reflection

  • In this data team project exploration, we collaborated with a company owned by a friend of mine in China to delve into e-commerce sales forecasting and inventory management.

    Throughout the project, in addition to the challenges faced by small cross-border e-commerce businesses, which we found in the literature to be the high cost of existing forecasting and inventory management systems, we discovered that the smaller the company, the more its unique characteristics become apparent. Our collaboration focused on selling home textile products, such as pillows and similar items.

    As members of the data science team, we engaged in multiple discussions based on actual data. Given the fine and diverse product categories with high turnover rates, data cleaning and exploratory data analysis (EDA) presented significant challenges. Fortunately, in the end, we successfully assisted our collaborator in implementing basic requirements and received positive feedback.

    Regarding this project, I have three main reflections:

    1. The importance of communication: A data scientist is not merely a data processor. Effective communication with clients involves understanding their specific problems, determining how I can assist them, and translating technical knowledge into layman's terms for effective communication. Communication with team members also plays a crucial role, ensuring alignment on the desired final outcome of our work. Overall, strong communication skills are essential for a data scientist.
    2. The urgency of self-improvement: While each team member has their own specialized skills and unicorn data scientists are quite rare, it is still necessary for me to continuously enhance my data science analysis abilities. While I may not be 100% proficient in every step, such as data cleaning, EDA, modeling, and development, having a solid understanding of at least 70% enables me to leverage my strengths in collaboration.
    3. Maintaining a positive mindset: Collaborating with a real-life partner exposed us to highly complex data, which significantly differed from the majority of datasets found on platforms like Kaggle. This often led to extensive discussions on the best approach. However, it is crucial to maintain a positive mindset and believe that we can make a difference, even if our solutions are simple. If we can genuinely help our clients, it is enough. This mindset will transform me into an outstanding data scientist through each small project I undertake.
